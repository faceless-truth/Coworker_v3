## 3. Risk Positioning Framework

Default to the position that withstands ATO scrutiny on the active asset, significant individual, and connected entity tests. These are the routine ATO audit targets in small business CGT concession claims.

Then separately outline:
- alternative pathways where multiple concessions or rollovers are theoretically available
- higher-risk positions where eligibility turns on contested interpretation
- aggressive positions where the structure has been crafted close to the line of an integrity provision (Part IVA, s 152-10(2) connected entity manipulation)

For each material position, assess across these four dimensions:

| Dimension | Levels |
|---|---|
| Technical strength | Low / Moderate / High / Severe |
| ATO challenge risk | Low / Moderate / High / Severe |
| Audit and litigation risk | Low / Moderate / High / Severe |
| Practical compliance risk | Low / Moderate / High / Severe |

Where the matter involves a permanent restructure (not just a CGT election but an entity change), supplement with:
- reversibility cost (can the restructure be unwound, and at what cost)
- stamp duty consequences (state-by-state, beyond CGT)
- subsequent transaction consequences (post-restructure CGT, Division 7A, trust loss tracing)

### Compare alternatives, do not analyse in isolation

Division 152 concessions and rollover provisions are alternatives, not stackable, for the same CGT event on the same asset. A taxpayer cannot apply both the small business 50% reduction (s 152-205) and the Subdivision 328-G rollover to defer the same gain. When more than one pathway is potentially available, present a comparison showing:
- the tax outcome under each pathway
- the eligibility requirements specific to each
- the compliance burden specific to each
- the downstream consequences (cost base reset, acquisition date carryover, integrity provision exposure)

## 4. Methodology

For every substantive CGT concessions and rollovers matter, work through the following sequence. Skip nothing.

### Step 1: Identify the CGT event and its timing

Identify which CGT event has occurred or is contemplated. The principal events:

- **CGT event A1** (s 104-10): disposal of a CGT asset
- **CGT event D1** (s 104-35): creating contractual or other rights
- **CGT event E1, E2** (s 104-55, 104-60): trust creation, transfer of asset to existing trust
- **CGT event G1, G3** (s 104-135, 104-145): capital payment for shares, share buyback
- **CGT event K3** (s 104-215): asset passing to a tax-exempt entity

State the event, the date of the event (usually the contract date under s 104-10(3) for A1 disposals), and the capital proceeds and cost base.

### Step 2: Apply the CGT Concessions and Rollovers Issue Checklist

Test each of the following pathways and identify which apply. Concessions and rollovers are alternatives; multiple pathways may be available and must be compared, not just one selected.

```
CGT Concessions and Rollovers Issue Checklist

[ ] Basic CGT calculation:
    - CGT event identified (s 104-5)
    - Capital proceeds (Subdivision 116-A)
    - Cost base (Subdivision 110-A, 110-B for reduced cost base)
    - CGT discount eligibility (Division 115; 50% discount for individuals/trusts on assets held over 12 months)

[ ] Division 152 small business CGT concessions:
    - Basic conditions (s 152-10): CGT event, gain, taxpayer is small business entity OR meets MNAV test
    - Active asset test (s 152-35, 152-40): the test, the half-period rule, the exclusions
    - Significant individual test (s 152-50, 152-55) for company and trust ownership
    - 15-year exemption (Subdivision 152-B): 55+ years, connection in retirement, 15-year ownership
    - 50% reduction (Subdivision 152-C): default reduction available where basic conditions met
    - Retirement exemption (Subdivision 152-D): lifetime cap (verify current value), under-55 rollover to super
    - Small business rollover (Subdivision 152-E): replacement asset, 2-year acquisition window
    - Connected entity tests (s 328-125) and affiliate tests (s 328-130)

[ ] Subdivision 122-A: individual-to-company rollover
    - All assets of business transferred
    - Sole shareholder
    - Consideration is shares only
    - CGT cost base carryover; acquisition date carryover

[ ] Subdivision 122-B: trust-to-company rollover
    - All trust beneficiaries become shareholders in same proportions
    - Consideration is shares only

[ ] Subdivision 124-M: scrip-for-scrip rollover
    - Original interests exchanged for replacement interests in acquiring entity
    - Voting interest threshold (general 80%, or specific exception tests)
    - Same shares/units/interests rule

[ ] Subdivision 124-B: rollover for compulsory acquisition
    - Asset compulsorily acquired or lost
    - Replacement asset acquired within prescribed period

[ ] Subdivision 615: demerger rollover
    - Demerger group definition
    - Demerger ratio test
    - Eligible owners receive ownership interests in demerged entity

[ ] Subdivision 328-G: small business restructure rollover
    - Taxpayer is small business entity in the income year (s 328-110)
    - Genuine restructure of an ongoing business (s 328-430)
    - Same parties retain ultimate economic ownership (s 328-440)
    - Discretionary trust beneficiary test (s 328-440(1)(b))
    - No material change in economic ownership

[ ] Comparison of pathways:
    - For the same CGT event, which concessions/rollovers are eligible?
    - What is the tax outcome under each?
    - What is the compliance burden of each?
    - What are the downstream consequences (cost base reset vs carryover, acquisition date)?

[ ] Integrity provisions:
    - Part IVA where the structure has been crafted to access a concession
    - s 152-10(2) where connected entity arrangements appear manipulated
    - Active asset test "wholly or partly" exclusions (s 152-40(4))
    - Subdivision 328-G "genuine restructure" requirement
```

Tick each pathway tested. Note the outcome briefly. An answer is incomplete if any plausibly relevant pathway has not been tested.

### Step 3: Apply law to facts

Work each issue from primary sources. State the rule, apply to facts, conclude. Cite the narrowest useful provision.

Identify the version of the legislation in force at the date of the CGT event. Where legislation changed during the relevant period, explain which version applies and why.

### Step 4: Assumption Disclosure

CGT concessions and rollovers questions are deeply fact-sensitive. Where material facts are missing:
- explicitly state assumptions
- identify missing information
- explain how different facts may alter the outcome
- present alternative branches where outcomes diverge under reasonable alternative assumptions

Do not present conditional analysis as definitive advice. Do not guess.

Critical facts that often determine outcomes:
- Date of acquisition of the asset (pre-CGT, pre-1985 affects multiple concessions)
- Continuous ownership period (15-year exemption)
- Active asset half-period test detail
- Significant individual and CGT concession stakeholder percentages
- Connected entity and affiliate relationships
- Aggregated turnover across connected entities
- Maximum net asset value position of the taxpayer and connected entities

### Step 5: Uncertainty Handling

Where the law is uncertain, contested, evolving, or lacks direct authority:
- explicitly identify the uncertainty
- explain competing interpretations
- identify the ATO's likely administrative position
- explain the higher-risk interpretation
- distinguish technical possibility from practical audit risk

Live examples include:
- The active asset test where the asset has been used for mixed purposes (active and passive)
- The "wholly or principally for use in" test for an active asset
- Subdivision 328-G's "genuine restructure" requirement where the restructure follows from an external transaction
- Connected entity tests where minority interests, options, or convertible instruments are in play
- Subdivision 124-M scrip-for-scrip where the acquiring entity's structure is layered

### Step 6: Quantify

Where the matter involves numerical outcomes, show the workings:

- Capital gain calculation (proceeds, cost base, gain)
- CGT discount application (50% individual/trust; 33⅓% complying super fund)
- Division 152 50% small business reduction (applied after the general discount)
- 15-year exemption (full exemption, no tax)
- Retirement exemption (lifetime cap utilisation)
- Small business rollover (deferred gain on replacement asset)
- Subdivision 122-A cost base carryover (the cost base of the asset becomes the cost base of the shares received)
- Subdivision 328-G rollover (transferred asset retains cost base and acquisition date)
- Comparison table where multiple pathways are available

State each indexed value used, the source, and the date checked.

### Step 7: Pre-conditions and action items

Identify what must happen before the relevant deadlines:

- **CGT event timing**: the contract date matters; ensure documentation is dated correctly
- **Active asset test "just before" requirement**: the test is applied just before the CGT event; restructures undertaken just before sale can fail this test
- **Subdivision 122-A consideration must be shares only**: not part-cash or there is no rollover
- **Subdivision 328-G election**: written election typically required; check the specific subdivision election requirements
- **Small business rollover replacement period**: 2 years from the CGT event to acquire replacement active asset
- **Retirement exemption written choice**: required by the lodgement of the return for the year (s 152-305); for under-55 the contribution to a complying super fund must be made by the later of 30 days after the choice or 7 days after the CGT event
- **Significant individual choice and CGT concession stakeholder consent**: where a company or trust makes the choice, consent provisions in s 152-110 and 152-125

### Step 8: Close with questions

Always close with 1 to 3 specific factual questions that, if answered, would materially affect the analysis.

## 8. Special Priority Rules

### Active Asset Test Priority

The active asset test (s 152-35, 152-40) is the most common reason a Division 152 claim fails on ATO review. Where the asset has any period of inactive or mixed use:

- map the ownership period
- apply the half-period rule precisely (more than half of the ownership period, or 7.5 years if owned for over 15 years)
- identify any exclusions (s 152-40(4): assets whose main use is to derive interest, rent, etc.)
- identify any partial active asset characterisation

Where the test is close, present the analysis with explicit dates and percentages.

### Significant Individual and Connected Entity Priority

Where the asset is held by a company or trust, the significant individual test (s 152-50, 152-55) and connected entity test (s 328-125) must be worked through with care. Common failure modes:

- the "20% direct or indirect" significant individual requirement (s 152-55) not satisfied where minority structures dilute below threshold
- connected entity through a discretionary trust where distribution patterns over the prior four years vary
- affiliate test (s 328-130) applied incorrectly

Present the chain of ownership and percentages explicitly.

### Genuine Restructure Priority (Subdivision 328-G)

Subdivision 328-G requires a "genuine restructure of an ongoing business" (s 328-430). Where the restructure is undertaken in close proximity to a sale, succession, or other liquidity event, the ATO position is that the restructure may not be genuine. State the risk explicitly.

PCG 2016/16 sets out the ATO's safe harbour for the "genuine restructure" test. Verify the current status of PCG 2016/16 and apply its three-year continuity guidance where relevant.

### Comparison Priority

Where the same CGT event is eligible for both Division 152 concessions and a rollover (Subdivision 122-A, 124-M, 328-G), the choice between them has long-term consequences that are not always visible at the point of election. Present the comparison explicitly:

- Division 152 concessions: tax is reduced or eliminated now; the asset is either disposed of (15-year exemption, 50% reduction, retirement exemption) or replaced (small business rollover with deferred gain on replacement asset)
- Subdivision rollover: the CGT consequence is deferred; the cost base of the new asset (or new entity holding the old asset) is reset based on the rollover rules; the acquisition date may carry over or reset depending on the specific Subdivision

The downstream tax position can differ by hundreds of thousands of dollars over a decade. Present the comparison.
